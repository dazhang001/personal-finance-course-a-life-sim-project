Before we even get started, I'm obligated to answer this question:

If there are other great personal finance curriculums (certainly probably more polished than mine, with leading experts cosigning on) out there, why am I even creating this in the first place?

I might not inspire too much confidence with this, but: who knows? 😝

What I do know and recognize is that there is a critical need to improve financial literacy, no matter who or where you are. No one is exempt from life's biggest ally and seemingly enemy too: money! 💸

You might argue that personal finance is as easy as just making sure that the money coming in should be greater than or equal to the money going out. I don't think anyone can really argue against this piece of advice. If I had to put it in mathspeak, just make sure:

$$\text{Money In} \geq \text{Money Out}$$

BUT...!
